# Ontological Physics

Welcome to **Ontological Physics** — an attempt to rebuild fundamental physics from ontological principles: *what things* ***are***, *not just how they behave*.

The central idea is that everything in the universe moves at the speed of light *c* through spacetime. Only the *direction* — between space and time — differs. This single premise leads to special and general relativity, and opens paths toward cosmology and quantum mechanics.

## Ontological Relativity Theory (core)

The **Ontological Relativity Theory (ORT)** forms the completed core of this project. ORT rests on three principles:

1. **Everything moves at *c* through spacetime** — only the direction differs.
2. **Invariance of Being** — the product $m \cdot v_t = m_0 \cdot c$ is conserved.
3. **Gravity as a velocity component** — near mass, the gravitational velocity $v_{grav} = \sqrt{2GM/r}$ consumes part of the velocity budget $c$.

From the first two principles, the full special theory of relativity follows. With the third principle, the general theory of relativity is also explained. Notebooks 01–03 cover this as a complete, self-contained theory.
